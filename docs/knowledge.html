Knowledge Base Reference Files

The Knowledge Base allows you to upload persistent reference materials to give your AI necessary background context.

Uploading Reference Materials

Your Knowledge Base is a persistent vector store of reference files (PDFs, Text, Markdown). These files are not analyzed in isolation; instead, they serve as "textbooks" that the AI consults when processing other dynamic documents.

Example Use Case: Upload a 50-page Vendor Compliance Manual to your Knowledge Base. When analyzing a newly uploaded vendor contract (from your Dashboard), you can inject the manual as Context. The AI will instantly cross-reference the active contract against the rules defined in your persistent manual.

Maintaining Templates

In addition to reference context, the Knowledge Base also acts as the central repository for your Templates. By uploading AcroForm PDFs or configuring a local designated Templates Folder, your physical company documents are idempotently synced and made available for the Template Fill engine without copying raw files to the database.

Context Length Limits: Injecting too many large Knowledge Base files as context into a single prompt may exceed your local LLM's Context Window (e.g., 8,000 tokens), causing it to crash or truncate text. If you have a massive Knowledge Base, use the Semantic Search or Keyword tools to narrow down the injection.
